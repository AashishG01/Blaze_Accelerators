# Blaze Challenge — Competitor Research: First-Salary Banking Landscape
## Research date: July 2026

---

## 1. Indian Banks — Salary Account Feature Comparison

| Bank | Core Offer | Interest Rate | First-Job-Relevant Features | Explicitly Missing |
|------|-----------|---------------|----------------------------|-------------------|
| HDFC | Zero-balance salary account (multiple tiers) | 3–3.5% p.a. | Instant OD up to ₹1.25L, free Platinum debit card, InvestNow robo-advisory | No AI guidance; OD not calibrated to first-jobber cash-flow |
| ICICI | Salary account (Silver/Gold/Titanium/M2O), 3-in-1 | 3–3.5% p.a. | 3-in-1 investment account, rewards, personal accident cover | Nothing beginner-oriented — assumes customer knows demat/trading |
| Axis | Prime/Prestige/Liberty/Easy Access | 3–3.5% p.a. | EDGE reward points, lounge access, dining discounts | Zero financial education or budgeting tooling |
| SBI | Zero-balance salary, largest branch network | Standard | Branch reach, OD facility | Weakest digital/app experience; no personalization |
| Kotak (811) | App-first zero-balance, ActivMoney sweep | Competitive | Fully digital onboarding, auto-FD sweep | No guided investing or literacy layer |
| IDFC FIRST | Positioned at "millennials," best savings rate | Up to 6.5% p.a. | ₹50L accident cover, lounge visits, zero fees on 28 services | Still a features list, not a journey — no "first salary" narrative |
| AU Small Finance | Digital-first SFB | Higher than average | Fast digital onboarding | Thin brand trust vs. majors |

**Pattern:** Every traditional bank presents a static bundle of perks identically to a 22-year-old first-jobber and a 45-year-old with a mortgage. None dynamically reacts to career stage. This is the exploitable gap.

## 2. Indian Fintechs / Neobanks

| Player | Core Offer | Standout Feature | Gap / Weakness |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Jupiter | Zero-balance, auto-categorized spend, "Pots" | Mini Loans up to ₹50K at 0% for salary holders | Overlaps with micro-credit wedge |
| CRED | Credit-card bill payment + rewards | Best-in-class gamification | **Hard wall**: requires CIBIL ~750+ — first-jobbers excluded |
| Niyo | Travel-forex neobank | Best 0% forex for international | Narrow use case, not primary banking |
| Groww/Zerodha/INDmoney | Investment-first platforms | Best investment UX | Not banking — no salary account |

### Critical Finding: Fi Money Shutdown

Fi Money (ex-Google Pay India leads, backed by Peak XV/Temasek, 3.5M users) shut down consumer banking on March 11, 2026. Revenue: ₹38Cr vs ₹301Cr losses (8:1 ratio). Validates: "beautiful app wrapped around partner bank" has no durable unit economics in India.

**Use in pitch:** "Why should the bank build this, not license from a fintech?" — Fi Money is the answer.

## 3. Feature Gap Matrix

| Customer need | Who offers it | Verdict |
|--------------|--------------|---------|
| Budgeting / auto-categorized spend | Jupiter, Monzo, HDFC (weakly) | **Saturated** — do not build as centerpiece |
| Rewards / cashback | Every bank + Jupiter + CRED | **Saturated and commoditized** |
| Investment onboarding | Groww, Zerodha, INDmoney | **Saturated** |
| Micro-credit against salary | Jupiter (0% mini-loans) | ⚠️ Not a clean gap — differentiate HOW |
| **Credit-history building as visible, gamified product** | **Nobody** | ✅ **Genuine gap — strongest differentiator** |
| **Dynamic life-stage-aware onboarding** | **Nobody** | ✅ **Genuine gap — cheap to prototype, hard to fake** |
| **Transaction-grounded AI advice** | **Nobody in India** | ✅ **Genuine gap — strongest AI justification** |

## 4. Customer Journey Pain Points

| Stage | Pain Point |
|-------|-----------|
| HR asks to open salary account | Zero choice — bank imposed, not chosen |
| Receives first salary | No moment marks it as significant |
| Spends freely | No friction, no framing — nothing says "this has to last 30 days" |
| Feels confused | Confusion has no destination inside the bank app — loses user to fintechs/Instagram |
| Needs guidance | Bank offers generic literacy content they won't read |
| Starts investing | On Groww/Zerodha, not the bank |
| Becomes loyal customer | To the fintech, not the bank |

---

## 5. Strategic Conclusions

1. **Kill "AI budget coach" as centerpiece** — it's table stakes, not a gap
2. **Credit-building as visible, gamified product** is the strongest differentiator — sell "the first entry in your credit history," not "a loan"
3. **Fi Money collapse** = live business-impact argument
4. **"Wedge, not menu"** is externally validated by every winning neobank (Chime, Wise, Nubank)
5. Screenshot targets for gap slides: Jupiter, HDFC, IDFC FIRST, CRED
